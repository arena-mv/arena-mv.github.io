Channel Dives

Adrenaline and action
Advanced

Cocoa Corner

Famous for its strong currents and pelagic action. Grey reef sharks patrol the edge while eagle rays glide through the blue.

12-30m Advanced

Highlights

Grey reef sharks, eagle rays, Napoleon wrasse, schooling jackfish, and occasional hammerheads in season.

Intermediate

Guraidhoo Corner

A thrilling drift dive along a steep wall with overhangs and caves. Perfect for spotting sharks and rays in the current.

10-25m Intermediate

Highlights

White tip reef sharks, eagle rays, turtles, and vibrant soft corals along the wall.

Advanced

Embudhoo Express

An exhilarating drift dive with strong currents pushing you along a dramatic wall. Reef hooks recommended.

15-30m Advanced

Highlights

Grey reef sharks, eagle rays, schools of barracuda, and occasional manta rays during plankton season.

Thilas & Pinnacles

Underwater mountains

Kandooma Thila

A submerged pinnacle rising from 30m to 15m below the surface. Famous for its vibrant soft corals and marine biodiversity.

15-30m Intermediate

Best for: Soft corals, schooling reef fish, occasional reef sharks, and turtles.

Maaya Thila

One of the most famous dive sites in the Maldives. This small pinnacle is packed with marine life from top to bottom.

8-30m All Levels

Best for: White tip reef sharks, moray eels, octopus, and stunning macro life. Excellent night dive.

Kuda Giri Thila

A small pinnacle with a beautiful coral garden on top and steep walls descending to 30m. Perfect for multi-level diving.

5-30m Beginner-Intermediate

Best for: Coral gardens, reef fish, turtles, and occasional eagle rays passing by in the blue.

Wreck Dives

Artificial reef habitats

The South Malé Atoll features several accessible wrecks that have transformed into thriving artificial reefs. From shallow training wrecks to deeper technical challenges, these sites offer unique marine life encounters.

Kuda Giri Wreck

A small cargo vessel sitting upright at 30m. Perfect for Advanced Open Water deep dives and wreck specialty training.

15-30m Intermediate

Maafushi Training Wreck

A shallow, purpose-sunk vessel perfect for beginners and underwater photography practice in controlled conditions.

5-12m Beginner

House Reef & Training Sites

Perfect for beginners

Maafushi House Reef

Just steps from our dive center, this protected reef offers ideal conditions for training, night dives, and relaxed exploration.

  • Depth: 5-18m
  • Experience: All levels
  • Highlights: Turtles, reef fish, rays

Biyadhoo Reef

A gentle sloping reef with excellent coral coverage and abundant marine life. Perfect for building confidence.

  • Depth: 5-15m
  • Experience: Beginner-Intermediate
  • Highlights: Coral gardens, reef sharks

Fihalhohi Reef

Protected lagoon with sandy patches and coral bommies. Ideal for skills practice and first open water experiences.

  • Depth: 3-12m
  • Experience: Beginner
  • Highlights: Stingrays, reef fish, macro life

Seasonal Highlights

When to see what

January - March

Peak visibility season with calm seas and minimal currents. Ideal for underwater photography and training.

Highlights: Whale sharks, eagle rays, reef sharks

April - May

Transition season with changing currents. Good mix of visibility and marine life activity.

Highlights: Manta rays begin appearing, shark activity increases

June - October

Southwest monsoon brings nutrient-rich waters. Slightly reduced visibility but maximum marine life.

Highlights: Manta ray aggregations, plankton feeders, shark action

November - December

Transition back to northeast monsoon. Improving visibility with still-active marine life.

Highlights: Whale sharks, hammerheads at deeper sites
