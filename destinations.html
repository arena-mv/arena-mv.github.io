Signature Dive Sites

Where we drop anchor

Cocoa Thila

The pinnacle that made South Malé famous. Expect grey reef sharks, schooling trevally, and electric currents.

  • Depth 18–30 m
  • Advanced drift dive
  • Best with incoming tide

Maafushi House Reef

Our backyard classroom. Perfect for Discover Scuba, night dives, and macro photography.

  • Depth 5–18 m
  • Hawksbill turtles daily
  • UV night dive upgrade

Guraidhoo Corner

Channel action with eagle rays, barracuda tornadoes, and manta cleaning stations in season.

  • Depth 12–30 m
  • Strong currents – experienced divers
  • Peak June–October

Our Daily Range

We cover a 20 km radius from Maafushi, reaching the best pinnacles, channels, and lagoon hideaways morning, noon, and night.

  • Dawn double-tanks hit the channels while currents are calmest.
  • Afternoon boats venture south to Vaavu crossovers for sharks and pelagics.
  • Sunset dhoni trips combine dolphin spotting with sandbank snorkels.

Beyond Scuba

Balance your surface time

Sandbank Picnics

Chef-prepared lunches on powder-blue islands with stand-up paddleboards and drone photography add-ons.

Ideal for rest days

Night Snorkel Safari

Glow-in-the-dark reef walks using UV torches, spotting octopus, lobsters, and coral fluorescence.

All equipment provided

Speedboat Manta Chase

Seasonal run north to Lankan Reef for manta snorkelling with naturalist briefings onboard.

Peak June–October

Visibility

20–30 m from December to April, 15–20 m during plankton-rich manta season.

Water Temps

27–29°C year-round. 3 mm wetsuits recommended for multiple daily dives.

Marine Highlights

Grey reef sharks, eagle rays, turtles, frogfish, leaf scorpion fish, and regular dolphin escorts.
